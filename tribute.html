Prof. Stephen Hawking

A lifetime of defying the odds

Here's a time line of Prof. Hawking's life:

  • 1942 - Born in Oxford, England
  • 1959 - Begins his studies at Oxford University at a very young age. His focus is on physics
  • 1962 - Leaves Oxford with a first-class honors degree and begins graduate work at Trinity Hall at Cambridge
  • 1963 - Gets diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS). This disease affects movement and speech. Hawking is told by medical professionals that he would only live for two more years
  • 1965 - Gets married to Jane Wilde. The two had met at a party in 1963
  • 1968 - Becomes a member of the Institute of Astronomy at Cambridge
  • 1969 - Needs the help of crutches, due to his health state. Hawking is later forced to use a wheelchair to move around
  • 1973 - Releases his first novel 'The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time'
  • 1975 - Receives the Pius XI Gold Medal for Science from Pope Paul VI.
  • 1977 - Becomes a professor at Cambridge. He serves as professor of Gravitational Physics
  • 1979 - Gets one of the most renowned teaching posts. Since 1663, the post of the Lucasian Professor of Mathematics had only been held by 14 people, including Sir Isaac Newton
  • 1982 - Gets awarded a CBE (Commander of the Order of the British Empire) by Queen Elizabeth II
  • 1985 - Loses his voice due to pneumonia. A developed speech synthesizing program later serves as his communication tool
  • 1988 - Releases 'A Brief History of Time', his most popular book. It sold over 25 million copies and was translated into 40 languages
  • 2009 - Receives the US Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian honor in America, from President Barack Obama
  • 2018 - Dies at the age of 76

“However bad life may seem, there is always something you can do and succeed at. Where there's life, there's hope.”

~ Stephen Hawking
